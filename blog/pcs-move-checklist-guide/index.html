PCS Move Checklist Guide: What to Do Before Your Military Relocation

July 13, 2026 | 8 min read | By Military Life Tools

PCS moves are stressful because the timeline is packed with orders, deadlines, paperwork, and family logistics. A good checklist can help reduce the chaos and make the process feel manageable.

Many service members search for a PCS move checklist because they need something practical and easy to follow. The key is to break the move into phases so you can focus on the most important tasks first.

What a good PCS checklist should include

  • Orders and move dates
  • Housing decisions and lease planning
  • Medical, school, and pet paperwork
  • Shipment and storage planning
  • Travel and temporary lodging logistics

Why planning early matters

Starting early helps you avoid last-minute surprises. If you begin planning 60 to 90 days before the move, you have more time to compare housing options, arrange travel, and confirm paperwork.

How to use the checklist tool

Use the PCS move checklist tool to generate a simple timeline based on your move date and branch. It can help turn a complex relocation into a manageable sequence of milestones.

Bottom line

The best PCS plans are simple, realistic, and started early. A checklist keeps the process organized so you can focus on the move without losing track of the essentials.
